About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate at the University of Minnesota Twin Cities, advised by Rahul Narain. My current research involves real-time elastic deformation with a focus on collision resolution. For a full list of research papers/talks, check out my CV above.

I received my M.S. and B.S. in Computer Science at the University of Minnesota Duluth. At UMD I was advised by Pete Willemsen and my thesis was on efficient simulations of urban heat.
